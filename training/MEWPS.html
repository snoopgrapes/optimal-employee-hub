Complete MEWP Safety Guide

This guide provides a comprehensive walkthrough of MEWP safety. Expand each section to see detailed instructions.

Introduction & MEWP Types

‚ñº
  • MEWP Types:
    • Boom lifts: Articulating or telescopic booms with platform (e.g., cherry pickers)
    • Scissor lifts: Vertical-only movement with large platform
    • Vertical personnel platforms: Compact lifts for vertical access
    • Trailer-mounted lifts: Portable units towed to location
  • Key Components:
    • Platform/basket: Working area with guardrails and gate
    • Boom/arms: Extendable sections for reach
    • Base/chassis: Contains engine, controls, and stabilizers
    • Controls: Ground and platform controls with emergency stop
    • Stabilizers/outriggers: For stability when extended
  • Classifications:
    • Group A: Vertical-only movement (e.g., scissor lifts)
    • Group B: Moving boom with platform (e.g., boom lifts)
    • Type 1: Driven only in stowed position
    • Type 2: Can be driven elevated on firm surfaces
    • Type 3: Can be driven elevated on rough terrain

Setup & Operation

‚ñº
  • Pre-Operation Checks:
    • Complete pre-start inspection checklist
    • Check fluid levels, tires, and structural components
    • Test all controls and safety devices
    • Verify emergency stop functions properly
    • Ensure all warning labels and capacity charts are legible
  • Site Assessment:
    • Identify overhead obstructions and power lines
    • Check ground conditions for stability and slope limits
    • Ensure adequate space for deployment and movement
    • Mark underground hazards (e.g., utility covers)
    • Establish exclusion zones around work area
  • Stabilization:
    • Deploy outriggers/stabilizers on firm, level surface
    • Use appropriate pads or mats on soft surfaces
    • Verify automatic leveling system is functioning
    • Never bypass interlocks or warning systems

Maintenance & Storage

‚ñº
  • Daily Maintenance:
    • Clean platform of debris and slippery substances
    • Check hydraulic systems for leaks
    • Inspect safety harness attachment points
    • Test all controls and emergency functions
    • Verify guardrails and gates function properly
  • Scheduled Maintenance:
    • Follow manufacturer's maintenance schedule
    • Lubricate moving parts as specified
    • Inspect structural components for cracks or damage
    • Check hydraulic hoses and fittings for wear
    • Verify all warning systems are operational
  • Storage:
    • Store in clean, dry location protected from weather
    • Lower platform fully and retract boom
    • Disconnect battery if storing for extended periods
    • Secure against unauthorized use
    • Keep maintenance records up to date

Safe Operation Procedures

Follow these procedures for safe use of MEWPs.

üîº

Basic Operation

  • Pre-use: Complete inspection checklist and wear harness
  • Entry/exit: Maintain 3-point contact when entering/exiting
  • Controls: Test ground and platform controls before use
  • Movement: Look in direction of travel and sound horn

Hazard Awareness

  • Overhead: Maintain 10+ ft clearance from power lines
  • Ground: Watch for slopes, holes, and unstable surfaces
  • Weather: Don't operate in high winds (>28 mph/12.5 m/s)
  • Traffic: Use spotters when moving near pedestrians/vehicles
üõ°Ô∏è

Platform Safety

  • Capacity: Never exceed maximum platform load rating
  • Positioning: Keep feet on platform floor at all times
  • Fall protection: Wear harness with lanyard attached to anchor
  • Materials: Secure tools and materials to prevent dropping
üîç

Emergency Procedures

  • Malfunction: Use emergency lowering procedure if stuck
  • Tip-over: Stay in platform - don't jump (survival space)
  • Power lines: If contact occurs, stay in platform and call for help
  • Rescue: Have emergency descent/rescue plan in place

Inspection & Problem Solving

Solution for: Structural Issues

Inspection Criteria: Check for bent or cracked boom sections, damaged platform components, worn pivot points, or excessive corrosion (>10% material thickness).

Solutions: Remove from service immediately. Tag as damaged. Structural repairs must be performed by manufacturer or certified technician. Most structural damage requires replacement of components.

Solution for: Hydraulic Problems

Inspection Criteria: Check for leaks at fittings, cylinders, and hoses. Listen for unusual pump noises. Check fluid levels and condition. Test for slow operation or drift.

Solutions: Tighten fittings if leaking. Replace damaged hoses. Change contaminated fluid. Bleed air from system. Major hydraulic repairs require qualified technician.

Solution for: Control Malfunctions

Inspection Criteria: Test all platform and ground controls. Check for sticking, delayed response, or uncommanded movement. Verify emergency stop functions.

Solutions: Clean and lubricate control linkages. Check electrical connections. Replace damaged control switches. Never bypass safety interlocks.

Solution for: Safety System Faults

Inspection Criteria: Test overload sensors, tilt alarms, limit switches, and guardrail interlocks. Verify audible alarms and warning lights function.

Solutions: Reset tripped sensors. Replace faulty switches. Repair damaged wiring. Safety systems must be fully operational before use - never bypass.

Safe Work Zones Around MEWPs

Critical information for working safely near operating MEWPs.

üöß

Exclusion Zones

  • Definition: Area around MEWP where only essential personnel are allowed
  • Size: Minimum 10 feet in all directions from platform (larger for high work)
  • Marking: Use cones, tape, or barriers with clear signage
  • Access: Only trained personnel assisting with the lift operation
üë∑

Ground Crew Safety

  • Positioning: Stay outside exclusion zones unless assisting
  • Awareness: Watch for moving equipment and overhead hazards
  • Communication: Maintain visual/radio contact with operator
  • Materials: Use tag lines to guide loads - never reach into danger zone
‚ö†Ô∏è

Overhead Hazards

  • Power lines: Maintain minimum 10 ft clearance (more for >50kV)
  • Structures: Watch for roof edges, beams, and other obstructions
  • Weather: Cease operations if lightning or high winds approach
  • Falling objects: Wear hard hats in vicinity of working MEWPs
üìã

Site Coordination

  • Planning: Include MEWP operations in site safety plans
  • Spotters: Use trained spotters when moving near hazards
  • Traffic: Establish pedestrian routes away from work zones
  • Signage: Post warning signs about overhead work

üìå Key Reminder:

Most MEWP accidents involve workers on the ground being struck by the equipment or falling objects. Always maintain awareness of MEWP operations in your work area and respect exclusion zones.
