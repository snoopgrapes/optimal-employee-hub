Understanding RAMS

RAMS stands for Risk Assessment and Method Statements. These are essential health and safety documents that identify hazards and outline safe working procedures.

What is a Risk Assessment?

‚ñº
  • Definition: A systematic process of identifying hazards and evaluating risks in the workplace.
  • Key Components:
    • Identifying potential hazards
    • Determining who might be harmed and how
    • Evaluating risks and existing control measures
    • Recording findings and implementing controls
    • Reviewing and updating as needed
  • Legal Requirement: Required under the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999.

What is a Method Statement?

‚ñº
  • Definition: A document that explains how a particular task or activity will be carried out safely.
  • Key Components:
    • Sequence of work operations
    • Specific hazards and control measures
    • Emergency procedures
    • PPE requirements
    • Training and competency requirements
  • Purpose: Provides clear instructions to workers and demonstrates safe systems of work to clients.

When Are RAMS Required?

‚ñº
  • For all work activities that pose significant risk
  • When required by clients or principal contractors
  • For complex or non-routine tasks
  • When working in high-risk environments (construction, manufacturing, etc.)
  • When new processes, equipment or materials are introduced
  • After accidents or near-misses to prevent recurrence

Employer & Employee Responsibilities

Clear understanding of responsibilities ensures compliance with health and safety legislation.

Employer Responsibilities

  • Legal Duty: Prepare suitable and sufficient risk assessments
  • Implementation: Ensure safe systems of work are followed
  • Training: Provide RAMS training to all employees
  • Resources: Supply necessary PPE and safety equipment
  • Review: Regularly review and update RAMS
  • Consultation: Consult with employees on health and safety matters

Employee Responsibilities

  • Compliance: Follow the safe working methods in RAMS
  • PPE: Use provided PPE correctly
  • Reporting: Report hazards and near misses
  • Training: Participate in required health and safety training
  • Cooperation: Cooperate with employer on safety matters
  • Accountability: Take reasonable care of own and others' safety

Creating Effective RAMS

Follow these steps to develop comprehensive and compliant RAMS documents.

Select a step from the list to view detailed instructions.

Step 1: Identify Hazards

Methods: Workplace inspections, task analysis, accident records, manufacturer information, consultation with employees.

Common Hazards: Slips/trips, manual handling, work at height, hazardous substances, machinery, electricity, noise, vibration, stress.

Consider: Who might be harmed? How might harm occur? Existing controls? Non-routine operations? Long-term health effects?

Step 2: Assess Risks

Risk = Likelihood √ó Severity

Rating Systems: Use a 3√ó3 or 5√ó5 matrix to evaluate risk levels (Low/Medium/High).

Factors: Frequency of exposure, number of people at risk, competence of workers, failure history of equipment.

Prioritization: Address highest risks first - those with severe potential consequences and likely to occur.

Step 3: Control Measures

Hierarchy of Controls:

  1. Elimination: Remove the hazard completely
  2. Substitution: Replace with less hazardous option
  3. Engineering: Isolate people from hazard
  4. Administrative: Change work practices
  5. PPE: Personal protective equipment

Document: Specify exactly how each hazard will be controlled in the method statement.

Step 4: Record Findings

Risk Assessment Format: Typically includes hazard description, who's at risk, existing controls, risk rating, additional controls required.

Method Statement Format: Scope of work, sequence of operations, PPE requirements, emergency procedures, training requirements.

Review Schedule: Set date for review (at least annually, or when work changes).

Distribution: Ensure all affected workers receive copies and understand content.
