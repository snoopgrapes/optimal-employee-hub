Preparation & Setup

‚ñº
  • Pre-Cleaning Assessment & Safety First:
    • Conduct a Site Survey and Risk Assessment: Identify glass type (e.g., coated, tinted, self-cleaning), assess existing condition (scratches, chips, residue), plan height and access, identify delicate surrounding areas, note obstacles, locate water source/drainage, and check weather conditions.
    • Communicate with Site Management: Confirm access permissions, water/power availability, coordinate work schedule, and establish a procedure for reporting pre-existing damage before starting.
  • Protection of Surfaces and People:
    • Protect the Immediate Area: Lay waterproof drop cloths or plastic sheeting on sills, floors, furniture, and adjacent surfaces. Consider low-tack painter's tape for delicate frames. Erect "Wet Floor" or "Work in Progress" signs and use barricades if needed.
    • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Wear safety glasses/goggles, chemical-resistant gloves, non-slip footwear, high-visibility clothing (on sites), a hard hat (if required), and a safety harness/lanyard (if working at height).
  • Equipment and Solution Preparation:
    • Gather and Check Equipment: Ensure you have clean buckets, correct squeegee sizes with sharp blades (and spares), an applicator/washer, scrapers (use with extreme caution, only on toughened glass, and wet), lint-free detailing cloths, non-abrasive sponges, stable ladders/access equipment, water-fed pole system (if applicable), vacuum, and rubbish bags.
    • Prepare Cleaning Solutions: Use a professional-grade, pH-neutral glass cleaner diluted per instructions. For heavy soiling, a mild, non-abrasive detergent can be used. Have specialty removers (solvent-based for sticky residue, acidic for mineral deposits if trained and safe). Always have clean water; de-ionized/purified water is best for streak-free results.
  • Initial Site Preparation and Debris Removal:
    • Clear the area by moving furniture or materials away from windows.
    • Brush or vacuum frames, sills, and tracks to remove loose dust and debris, preventing scratches.
    • Carefully remove any labels, tape, or stickers using a plastic scraper or appropriate solvent after testing.
    • Perform an Initial Scrape if necessary for dried mortar or paint, using a sharp, fresh blade at a very shallow angle on a *wet* surface, never on dry glass. Exercise extreme caution, especially on coated glass, and note that many contractors avoid scrapers on modern glass due to damage risk.

Cleaning Procedure

‚ñº
  • Initial Cleaning (Builders' Clean - Focus on Removing Construction Residue):

    This stage is about removing the bulk of the construction-related grime.

    • Wet the Glass Thoroughly:
      • Using your applicator/washer, liberally wet the entire glass surface with your pre-mixed cleaning solution. Ensure all areas are covered. This helps to loosen dirt and lubricate the surface for scraping.
      • For extremely dirty glass, you might need to apply the solution, let it dwell for a minute or two (without drying), and then re-wet.
    • Agitate and Loosen Dirt:
      • Vigorously scrub the glass with your wet applicator, paying special attention to areas with visible dirt, plaster, or paint splashes.
      • Work the solution into all corners and edges.
    • Address Stubborn Residues (with extreme caution):
      • Scraping (if necessary and safe): If, after wetting, there are still stubborn specks of paint, silicone, mortar, or plaster, and only if you are absolutely certain the glass type is safe for scraping (e.g., standard annealed toughened glass, not coated or delicate types):
        • Always keep the glass very wet.
        • Use a fresh, sharp razor blade scraper at a very low angle (almost flat, about 10-15 degrees) to gently lift the residue.
        • Work in one direction, not back and forth.
        • Wipe the blade frequently to remove debris.
        • Important Note: The safest practice, especially with modern glass, is to avoid scrapers if possible. Many scratches are caused by improper scraping or microscopic debris caught under the blade. If in doubt, do not scrape.
      • Specialty Removers: For adhesives, silicone, or paint, apply the appropriate solvent or chemical remover (tested previously on an inconspicuous area) directly to the residue using a clean cloth. Let it dwell for the recommended time, then gently rub off. Re-wet the glass area with your standard cleaning solution immediately after to wash away chemical residue.
      • Non-Abrasive Pads: For general grime that's stuck but not requiring scraping, a non-abrasive white or blue scrub pad (designed for glass) can be used with your solution.
    • Clean Frames and Sills:
      • Before squeegeeing the glass, thoroughly clean the window frames, sills, and tracks. This prevents dirty water from dripping onto your newly cleaned glass.
      • Use a clean cloth and your general cleaning solution (or a separate all-purpose cleaner if frames are heavily soiled). Pay attention to removing paint drips, plaster dust, and general construction grime.
      • For uPVC frames, a PVC cleaner can be effective, but always test first.
      • Wipe out tracks with a damp cloth or vacuum if needed.
  • Squeegeeing for a Streak-Free Finish:

    This is the key to professional-looking windows.

    • Prepare Your Squeegee:
      • Ensure the rubber blade is clean and free of nicks or debris. A damaged blade will leave streaks.
      • If using a traditional squeegee, ensure the channel is securely fastened.
    • Overlap Method:
      • Top Edge First: Start by squeegeeing a clean strip across the very top of the window, about an inch from the frame. This creates a "dry edge" to work from.
      • Downward Strokes (or Side-to-Side):
        • Option A (Professional/Faster): Overlapping Downward Strokes: Place the squeegee on the dry edge at the top corner. Pull it down in a straight, overlapping stroke, ensuring the squeegee blade slightly overlaps the previous stroke (about 1-2 inches).
        • Option B (Common/Easier): Side-to-Side Overlapping: Start at the top corner. Pull the squeegee horizontally across the top, pivot slightly, and pull down diagonally to create a fanning motion, overlapping each previous stroke.
      • Maintain Constant Pressure: Apply consistent, even pressure across the entire blade. Too much pressure can cause drag; too little can leave streaks.
      • Angle: Hold the squeegee at a slight angle (around 15-20 degrees) to the glass, allowing the dirty water to run down ahead of the blade.
      • Wipe the Blade: After each stroke, wipe the squeegee blade clean with a clean microfiber cloth. This prevents transferring dirt back onto the glass.
    • Bottom Edge and Corners:
      • Ensure you squeegee right down to the bottom frame.
      • For the very bottom edge, you may need to use a clean detailing cloth to absorb any remaining water or a small squeegee for tight spots.
  • Detailing and Final Inspection (Sparkle Clean Focus):

    This stage is about perfection and ensuring no residue is left behind.

    • Detail the Edges and Corners:
      • Immediately after squeegeeing each section, use a clean, dry, lint-free detailing cloth (microfiber or huck towel) to wipe the perimeter of the window where the squeegee couldn't reach perfectly.
      • Wipe off any drips or streaks on the frames or sills.
      • Change detailing cloths frequently as they become damp or dirty.
    • Inspect for Streaks and Smudges:
      • Step back and visually inspect the glass from different angles and in varying light conditions (if possible).
      • Look for any streaks, smudges, or missed spots.
      • If you find any, re-wet the affected area with your solution and re-squeegee or carefully wipe with a clean, dry detailing cloth.
    • Clean Interior Glass:
      • Repeat the entire process for the interior side of the glass. Pay extra attention to protecting interior finishes.
    • Final Wipe-Down of Frames and Sills:
      • Once the glass is pristine, give a final wipe-down to the window frames, sills, and ledges to ensure they are completely clean and dry, removing any watermarks or leftover residue.
  • Post-Cleaning Procedures:
    • Remove Protection: Carefully roll up or gather your drop cloths and sheeting, ensuring not to spread any collected debris.
    • Waste Disposal: Dispose of all waste, including used cleaning cloths, tape, and labels, in designated rubbish bins.
    • Clean Equipment: Rinse out buckets, clean squeegee blades, wash applicator sleeves, and hang cloths to dry. Proper equipment maintenance prolongs their life and ensures readiness for the next job.
    • Report Issues: Document and report any issues encountered during cleaning, such as pre-existing damage, persistent staining, or any problems with the window itself, to the site manager.

Post-Cleaning

‚ñº
  • Clean up any drips or spills on surrounding surfaces.
  • Rinse all tools (squeegee, scrubber, buckets) with clean water.
  • Dry tools thoroughly to prevent rust and mildew.
  • Store all cleaning supplies in a designated, clean, and dry area.
  • Replace any moved furniture or items.

Solution for: Streaks Left on Glass

Possible Causes: Worn/damaged squeegee blade, dirty squeegee blade, improper squeegee angle, too much or too little solution, dirty cloths.

Solutions: Replace worn blade, clean blade thoroughly, adjust squeegee angle to 45 degrees, use correct solution ratio, use clean lint-free cloths.

Solution for: Drips or Excessive Water Runoff

Possible Causes: Too much cleaning solution applied, squeegee not wiped clean between passes, squeegee angle incorrect.

Solutions: Use less solution, wipe squeegee blade after *every* stroke, ensure proper squeegee angle.

Solution for: Glass Appears Hazy After Cleaning

Possible Causes: Solution not rinsed off adequately, residue from cleaning solution, using soap that leaves residue, dirty water in bucket, fabric softener residue on cloths.

Solutions: Ensure thorough squeegee passes, use a streak-free cleaning solution, change water frequently, avoid fabric softener on cloths.

Solution for: Difficulty Removing Stubborn Grime

Possible Causes: Insufficient soaking time, wrong cleaning solution for the type of grime, or requires gentle scraping.

Solutions: Allow solution to soak for a few minutes, use a stronger or specialized glass cleaner for tough stains (e.g., hard water spots), carefully use a non-abrasive scraper for caked-on debris (e.g., paint, tape residue).
