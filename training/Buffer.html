Pre-Operation Safety & Setup

‚ñº
Pre-Operation Inspection:
  • Inspect power cord and plug for damage, cuts, or exposed wires
  • Check handle for secure attachment and proper operation
  • Examine pad driver for wear, damage, or loose attachment
  • Verify motor housing is intact with no cracks or damage
  • Test switch operation before connecting to power
Personal Protective Equipment:
  • Slip-resistant safety shoes (required)
  • Safety goggles to protect from debris
  • Hearing protection for extended operation
  • Long pants to protect legs from debris
  • Avoid loose clothing that could catch in machinery
Work Area Preparation:
  • Remove all furniture, equipment, and obstacles from work area
  • Clear floor of loose debris, cords, and small objects
  • Ensure adequate lighting throughout work area
  • Post appropriate signage ("Floor Burnishing in Progress")
  • Verify proper ventilation, especially in enclosed spaces

Pad Selection & Installation

‚ñº
Pad Selection Guide:
  • Ultra High-Speed Pads (White/Tan): For high-gloss burnishing and polishing
  • High-Speed Pads (Pink/Red): For light cleaning and polishing
  • Scrubbing Pads (Blue/Green): For removing scuff marks (use with caution)
  • Natural Hair Pads: For delicate surfaces requiring gentle polishing
Safe Pad Installation:
  • Always turn off and unplug machine before pad changes
  • Carefully tilt machine onto its side on a stable surface
  • Center pad precisely on the driver plate
  • Press down firmly around entire circumference
  • Ensure pad is completely flat with no wrinkles or air bubbles
  • Return machine to upright position slowly and carefully
Pad Condition Check:
  • Inspect pad for wear, tears, or embedded debris
  • Replace pads when worn down to 50% original thickness
  • Remove any foreign objects stuck to pad surface
  • Ensure pad is appropriate for the floor type being burnished

Post-Operation & Storage

‚ñº
  • Turn off machine and unplug immediately after use
  • Allow motor to cool completely before storage
  • Remove and inspect burnishing pad for wear
  • Clean pad driver and remove any debris buildup
  • Wipe down machine housing with clean, damp cloth
  • Inspect power cord and coil properly for storage
  • Store in designated area away from moisture and extreme temperatures
  • Report any equipment issues or damage to supervisor immediately
  • Complete maintenance log if required by facility protocols
